Academic Work

My coursework in the Higher Education Administration program has pushed me to examine the systems students navigate — and to think critically about how those systems can better support the people inside them. Below are highlights from four courses, each paired with a project or resource I developed along the way.

Higher Education Law

Created a video presentation examining legal frameworks for student accessibility and accommodation requirements, exploring how institutions navigate compliance with the ADA and Section 504 while supporting diverse student needs.

Watch Presentation

Note: This video is hosted on W&M's Panopto platform and may require a William & Mary login to view.

Assessment and Evaluation to Promote College Student Learning

Developed a comprehensive assessment proposal for evaluating accessibility office effectiveness, applying assessment frameworks to real-world student support services. The project focused on measuring whether accommodations for students with cognitive disabilities are individualized rather than defaulting to standardized supports like extended testing time.

View Assessment Proposal
Leadership and Change in Higher Education

Designed a leadership development intervention for peer mentorship programs, focusing on practical training structures for student leaders. The project, Demystifying College, applies adaptive leadership theory to help peer advisors distinguish between technical questions and adaptive challenges in advising conversations.

View Leadership Learning Intervention
Current Events in Higher Education

Researched and presented on college reading comprehension challenges, connecting national trends to practical interventions. This work directly informed the development of a reading strategies workshop I later facilitated as Lead Coach — addressing the gap between high school and college-level reading expectations.
