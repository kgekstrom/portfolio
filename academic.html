Academic Work

My coursework in the Higher Education Administration program has pushed me to examine the systems students navigate — and to think critically about how those systems can better support the people inside them. Below are highlights from four courses, each paired with a project or resource I developed along the way.

Higher Education Law
  • Examined legal frameworks governing student accessibility, including ADA and Section 504 compliance requirements
  • Analyzed institutional obligations in providing disability accommodations while navigating privacy, liability, and equity concerns
  • Created video presentation exploring how legal requirements intersect with practical challenges in supporting students with cognitive disabilities
  • Connected legal concepts to real-world accessibility office practices from my SAS graduate assistantship

Assessment and Evaluation to Promote College Student Learning
  • Developed comprehensive assessment proposal for evaluating Student Accessibility Services, specifically examining whether accommodations for students with cognitive disabilities are individualized or defaulted to standard supports like extended testing time
  • Applied mixed-methods design combining Likert-scale survey questions and open-ended responses to assess student experiences
  • Centered equity considerations by disaggregating data across identity groups to identify potential disparities in accommodation quality
  • Designed assessment with actionable recommendations for closing the loop between student feedback and service improvement
View Assessment Proposal
Leadership and Change in Higher Education
  • Analyzed adaptive leadership theory and leadership identity development frameworks
  • Designed Demystifying College, a four-workshop leadership learning intervention for peer mentorship programs that reframes peer advising as facilitation rather than information delivery
  • Applied theoretical frameworks to practical skill-building, teaching peer advisors to distinguish between technical questions and adaptive challenges, surface hidden curriculum, and transfer decision-making ownership to students
  • Connected leadership theory to equity and access by examining how hidden curriculum gaps disproportionately affect first-generation, transfer, and non-traditional students
View Leadership Learning Intervention
Current Events in Higher Education
  • Researched the "college reading crisis," examining why students struggle with dense academic texts and how high school preparation gaps create barriers to academic success
  • Presented hour-long workshop analyzing national trends in reading comprehension challenges and exploring practical interventions
  • Applied research findings directly to my work as Lead Coach, developing and facilitating a workshop on college-level reading strategies for students
  • Connected national higher education trends to actionable, student-facing programming
